# Promotional Material

## Short Positioning

PLI Labs publishes practical B70-class research for local legal AI. The work
shows how to validate a non-CUDA owned-hardware lane without confusing model
load success with production readiness.

## Longer Positioning

B70-class hardware is compelling because it changes the economics of private
local AI. The public lesson is not an exact launch recipe. The lesson is that a
validated GGUF-compatible SYCL lane can support serious legal drafting and
review experiments when measured against realistic legal workloads.

PLI's view is simple: local legal AI should be measurable, private, and
auditable. A credible public release should explain the model family, workload
tier, backend family, validation status, and privacy boundary without exposing
deployment-specific operations.

## Public Claims

- B70-class hardware is a serious non-CUDA research lane for private legal AI.
- GGUF-compatible SYCL serving can be a reliable capacity path.
- Long-context legal prompts are better promotion gates than short toy tests.
- Tensor-parallel XPU serving remains a separate research path.
- Public benchmark claims should use rounded bands and clear validation status.
