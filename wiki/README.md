# PLI Labs Wiki

This wiki is the public explanation layer for PLI Labs releases. It turns the
private research notes, website copy, and launch-talk thesis into clean public
materials that a lawyer, researcher, or local-model user can understand.

## The Release Thesis

PLI Labs exists because legal AI needs more than generic benchmarks and generic
compression. Legal work stresses:

- source-grounded long-form writing;
- careful distinction between facts, inferences, and advocacy;
- retrieval over prior work product and source evidence;
- privacy-preserving local deployment;
- lawyer-verifiable outputs instead of autonomous final answers.

The PLI launch talk framed the durable asset as proprietary legal intelligence:
the combination of prompts, inputs, source materials, workflow choices, human
corrections, and final lawyer-approved outputs. That idea drives this public
repo. A model release should say what legal behavior it tried to preserve and
how that behavior was checked.
